Fire Support Group


2nd 1st battalion

"You bump it, we thump it"

Below is each weapon system used by FSG platoon, each weapon system differs in size and calibre but ultimately works towards the same goal of either suppresive fire or elimination of armoured vehicles.

SFMG

SFMG - sustained fire machine gun is a belt fed machine gun capable of firing 900 rounds per minute. This machine gun fires a calibre of 7.62mm rounds and is able to provide direct fire support up to 1800m and up to 2500m of indirect fire. Firing this weapon at 20 round bursts creates a large beaten zone making it perfect for anti-personnel fire.

The total weight of this weapon system is 26KGs

GMG

GMG - Grenade machine gun is a fully automatic, belt fed, blow-back operated weapon, capable of a sustained high volume of fire in single rounds or bursts.
It is simple and sturdy in construction, easy to learn and fire. Stoppages are rare and can be easily and quickly remedied.

    Other characteristics are:
  • It can be fitted with a variety of sights for use by both day and night.
  • the GMG team usually consists of two or three men, however it can be loaded and fired by one man if necessary.
The total weight of this weapon system is 63.74 KGs

HMG 50. cal

HMG - 50 calibre browning machine gun is a short recoil heavy machine gun capable of firing up to 2500m effectively. The heavy calibre of this weapon makes it extremely effective not only against personnel but armoured vehicles, bunkers and low flying aircraft. Although this weapon is only fired at 3-5 round bursts the magnitude of the impact is still extremely effective for most tasks.

The total weight of this weapon system is 58KGs

Anti-Armour platoon

FSG consists of another element within their sub-unit that specialises in the destruction or disabling of armoured vehicles and helicopters. This element is known as Anti-Armour platoon.

Javelin Missile

Javelin missile is a fire-and-forget anti-tank missile that utilises an infrared guidance that allows the firer to detect any vehicle emitting a heat signature. The javelin missile can engage targets out to 2500m using either direct attack or top attack. The javelin's high explosive anti-tank (HEAT) warhead can defeat modern tanks with the top attack where armour is thinnest.
The javelin's time of flight is extremely fast reaching 2000m in 14 seconds making it difficult for armoured vehicles to defend against.

The total weight of this weapon system is 22.3KGs

AMR

AMR - Anti-material rifle (Barrett M107a1) is a semi automatic, recoil operated weapon capable of effective fire up to 1500m. The AMR is designed to pierce through light armoured vehicles, bunkers and buildings. The high powered optics mounted on the M107A1 provide soldiers with enhanced targeting capabilities and situational awareness. This rifle is easily carried and operated by a single soldier. To meet mission requirements it can be used with a range of ancillaries such as: night vision equipment, suppressor, auxiliary aiming devices, ballistic computer, and a tripod.

The total weight of this weapon is 16KGs

Transport

Most times light infantry mobilise on foot, but some task require the use of a vehicle with one of the weapons mounted on top.

Pinzguaer

The Pinzgauer is an off-the-shelf light military vehicle that is used to carry out transport, command and control, liaison, replenishment, and administrative tasks. This vehicle is utilised only during training and is not deployed overseas for combat. There is an armoured variant of the vehicle that would be utilised on operations.
